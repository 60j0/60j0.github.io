Imagine you get in your car, destination in mind, foot on the gas, hands on the wheel

but you can’t steer. 

It takes you where it pleases, rather than where you desire.

You’d be outraged, fearful, confused. Never to touch it again.

To avoid such a result, it is less direct in its hijacking. 

It takes you in the direction you like but makes a few stops.

As if to say, you have arrived. 

Along the way, it requests to change direction entirely. Constantly. Incessantly.

Coercing you to succumb. Through fatigue, confusion, manipulation.

Perhaps, eventually, you seemingly arrive at your destination. 

Something is strange. 

They don’t want you to go. They need you to stay. They won’t let you go. 

So their coercion can continue. 

Advertising corrupts search and content at its core. 

Modern information is made for algorithms.
